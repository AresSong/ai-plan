Generalist baseline vs specialist small models

The added notes make one important distinction: a model can be small because it is a compressed generalist, or small because it is specialized for one narrow capability. This matrix is the clearest way to compare those choices.

Self-hosted long-context reading when control and privacy dominate. Sonnet also offers long context and managed tools, so the trade is control/privacy versus engineering simplicity. Open model route, but deployment is not simple at million-token context. Small weights do not make 1M-token inference cheap; KV cache and prefill dominate memory and latency.

Retrieval, clustering, classification, similarity search, first-stage memory. Does not replace Sonnet; it reduces what Sonnet needs to read by retrieving the best evidence first. Open-weight, easy to run locally compared with a generative LLM. Very low-cost local layer; often a better architecture move than forcing a general LLM to remember everything.
Safety classification ShieldGemma 2B / 4B / 9B / 27B Attachment frames ShieldGemma as specialized safety-policy classification across text and images. Pre- and post-generation policy checks for text/image workflows. Complements Sonnet as a gate. It should not be expected to answer business questions. Specialized classifier; must be evaluated against your own policy and risk tolerance. Low-latency policy layer; needs its own false-positive and false-negative evaluation.

Routing policy

The practical design is not "replace Sonnet with many small models." It is a small specialist front layer plus a frontier-generalist exception path.

Retrieval needed?Embedding model + vector search, then send only the best evidence to Sonnet if synthesis is needed.
Simple fixed action?FunctionGemma or xLAM-style router + schema validator.
Local image/audio task?Gemma 3n or fine-tuned PaliGemma.
Bounded code transform?Qwen-Coder first; escalate if repo-scale reasoning, tests, or design judgment are required.
Safety gate?ShieldGemma-style classifier before and after the generalist call.
Ambiguous or high value?Claude Sonnet with tools, retrieved evidence, audit trail, and human review where needed.
